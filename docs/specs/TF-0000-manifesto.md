# TF-0000: Manifesto

## Status

Draft.

## Manifesto

The future of authentication is not login.

The future is verifiable action by cryptographic actors over authenticated channels.

Software is entering an era where humans are no longer the only meaningful actors. AI agents invoke tools, services talk to services, devices make decisions, relays forward messages, plugins transform identity, models influence actions, and systems operate across local, cloud, embedded, offline, and federated environments.

Old authentication asks:

> Did someone present the right secret?

TrustForge asks:

> What entity is acting, through what authority, under what constraints, through what channel, with what proof, and with what revocation path?

## Doctrine

TrustForge exists to create a shared trust fabric where every actor can be identified, every actor instance can be distinguished, every session can be authenticated, every transport can be bound to trust, every permission can be scoped, every denial can be explicit, every delegation can be traced, every approval can be proven, every high-risk action can be governed, every important event can be verified, and every integration can be made legible to humans and AI systems.

## Non-negotiable direction

TrustForge should be open-source, Rust-first, binary-first, AI-native, transport-agnostic, crypto-agile, post-quantum/hybrid-ready, offline-capable, mesh-capable, plugin-extensible, policy-driven, proof-oriented, conformance-tested, and compatible with existing standards.

## Warning

TrustForge must not become custom crypto.

TrustForge should compose reviewed primitives and existing standards into a new protocol architecture.
